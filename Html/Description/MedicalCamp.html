About

In Indian spiritual traditions, it is believed that the body is a temple that houses the soul. The physical body is considered a valuable asset that enables one to perform good deeds, practice spiritual disciplines, and contribute to society. Therefore, maintaining good health and well-being is considered essential for spiritual growth and enlightenment.

Medical seva, then, is an act of compassion towards others that involves providing healthcare services without any expectation of reward or recognition. It is based on the principle of seva or selfless service, which is considered a fundamental aspect of many Indian spiritual traditions.

Through medical seva, one is able to serve others in need, alleviate their suffering, and promote their well-being. It is seen as a way of expressing gratitude towards the universe for the gift of good health and acknowledging the interconnectedness of all living beings. It is also believed that by performing such acts of compassion, one can develop a sense of humility, empathy, and compassion towards others, which are essential qualities for spiritual growth and enlightenment.

In essence, medical seva represents the highest form of charity, where the act of providing healthcare services becomes a means for promoting social harmony, compassion, and spiritual growth.
